# Restaurant Market Analysis & Expansion Strategy

An **Excel-based** market intelligence case study identifying restaurant expansion opportunities across major Indian cities analyzing restaurant density, cuisine demand, customer ratings, and pricing to recommend where and how a restaurant brand should expand.

---

## Project Overview

Where should a restaurant brand open next? This case study answers that question with data — analyzing **8,799 restaurants** across **9 major Indian cities**, **3 price segments**, and **13 cuisine categories** to build a data-driven scoring framework for expansion planning, built entirely in **Excel**.

The final output is a market intelligence report recommending specific cities and cuisine segments with strong growth potential.

---

## Business Problem

A restaurant brand planning expansion wants to know:
- Which cities have the strongest market opportunity
- Which cuisines are in high demand vs. underserved
- How pricing strategy should be positioned for market entry
- Where competition is low but customer demand/quality signals are strong
- Which cities show quality gaps worth capitalizing on

---

## Data Source

- **Dataset:** Kaggle
- **Records:** 8,799 restaurants
- **Coverage:** 9 major Indian cities
- **Fields used:** City, cuisine category, price range/segment, customer rating, restaurant count

## Analysis Structure

1. **Market Overview** — total restaurants, average rating, average price across 9 cities
2. **City-Wise Restaurant Count & Rating** — restaurant density vs. average rating by city
3. **Cuisine Demand Analysis** — restaurant volume and rating by cuisine category
4. **Pricing Segment Analysis** — distribution across Budget, Mid-Range, and Premium segments
5. **Expansion Recommendations** — final city and cuisine recommendations based on combined scoring

---

## Key Insights

- **Kolkata** leads in both restaurant density and average rating, making it the strongest overall market
- **Delhi** ranks 8th in density but shows a concerningly low average rating  a quality red flag
- **North Indian** dominates cuisine demand (2,500+ listings) but is highly competitive, while **South Indian** earns the highest average rating despite lower volume — a quality niche opportunity
- **Mexican and Middle Eastern** cuisines show strong ratings with low competition — underserved niche markets worth watching
- **Surat** is an underserved gem: lowest restaurant density but a rating that signals real quality demand
- **Ahmedabad** shows moderate density with below-average ratings — an opportunity if quality gaps are addressed

---

## Final Recommendations

- **Best Cities for Expansion:** Ahmedabad, Bangalore, and Surat — lower competition with room to dominate on quality
- **High-Demand Cuisines:** Combine North Indian (reach) with South Indian (satisfaction) for the best of both worlds
- **Optimal Pricing:** Enter with a Budget menu to capture volume, then upsell into the fast-growing Mid-Range segment
- **Underserved Markets:** Surat and Delhi stand out as prime targets for a quality-focused new entrant

---

## Tools & Techniques Used

- **Microsoft Excel** — data cleaning, pivot tables, formulas (AVERAGEIFS, COUNTIFS, etc.)
- **Excel Charts** — bar charts, comparative visualizations for city and cuisine analysis
- **Market Scoring Framework** — combined density, rating, and pricing signals to rank expansion opportunities

---

## Business Impact

This analysis turns raw restaurant data into a clear go-to-market roadmap — helping decision-makers prioritize cities, cuisines, and pricing strategy to maximize the odds of a successful expansion while minimizing competitive risk.
